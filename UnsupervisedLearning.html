Unsupervised Learning

What is unsupervised learning? Well, it’s pretty self-explanatory: learning without a teacher.

No labels needed to be provided by teacher like in supervised learning.

The most basic way to model the world: assume that it is made up of distinct

groups of objects that share certain properties, and that's what unsupervised learning assumes.

Immediately recognizing properties and creating characteristics is known as unsupervised clustering

Important assumption in unsupervised clustering: certain objects are more similar than others.

Two key questions:

● What observations can we measure?

● How do we want to represent the world?

K-means clustering algorithm:

The data is put into groups called K clusters

There are two steps that occur:

● Predict

● Learn
